üìú Introduction

Hanuman Prashnavali is a sacred method of seeking divine guidance, deeply rooted in Hindu traditions. Devotees believe that Pavanputra Hanuman, the greatest devotee of Lord Rama, blesses those who seek answers through this system with his divine wisdom and protection.

"Jai Hanuman Gyan Gun Sagar, Jai Kapis Tihu Lok Ujagar"

This powerful verse from the Hanuman Chalisa describes Hanuman Ji as an ocean of wisdom and virtue, illuminating all three worlds with his divine presence. Through the Prashnavali system, devotees can tap into this infinite ocean of wisdom to seek guidance for their life's challenges and questions.

üîÜ The Divine Messenger: Lord Hanuman

Hanuman stands as one of the most revered deities in Hinduism, embodying strength, devotion, humility, and wisdom. Born to Anjani and blessed by the Wind God Vayu, Hanuman possesses extraordinary powers and abilities that he uses solely in service of the divine and the welfare of devotees.

1Ô∏è‚É£ Birth and Early Life of Hanuman

The birth of Hanuman is itself a divine story filled with miraculous events. Anjani, his mother, was blessed by Lord Shiva and received a divine portion of sacred pudding (payasam) which led to the birth of Hanuman. As a child, mistaking the rising sun for a ripe fruit, Hanuman leaped towards it, showcasing his extraordinary abilities from an early age.

This incident led to a series of blessings and curses from various deities: - The Sun God blessed him with wisdom - Indra struck him with his vajra, leading to his famous jaw (Hanuman) - Lord Brahma and other gods blessed him with multiple divine powers - Lord Shiva blessed him with immortality and invincibility

2Ô∏è‚É£ Hanuman in Ramayana

Hanuman's role in the Ramayana stands as the greatest testament to his devotion and capabilities. His meeting with Lord Rama marked the beginning of the most celebrated divine friendship in Hindu mythology. As Ram's devoted servant, he: - Crossed the mighty ocean to find Sita in Lanka - Carried the entire Sanjeevani mountain to save Lakshmana - Led the monkey army in the war against Ravana - Served as a messenger between Ram and Sita

3Ô∏è‚É£ Hanuman as Chiranjeevi

As one of the seven immortals (Chiranjeevis) in Hindu mythology, Hanuman continues to exist in our world, blessing devotees and guiding them through their spiritual journey. His presence is particularly strong in this Kali Yuga, where he helps devotees overcome obstacles and find their path to righteousness.

üåü Origins of Hanuman Prashnavali

The Hanuman Prashnavali system emerged from the deep meditation and spiritual practices of ancient sages who sought to create a bridge between the divine wisdom of Hanuman and the everyday needs of devotees. The system was revealed through divine inspiration and has been preserved through generations of spiritual practitioners.

The significance of 49 messages in the Prashnavali relates to: - The seven chakras of spiritual energy - The seven planes of consciousness - The seven days of the week - The seven primary colors of light Each number thus resonates with multiple layers of spiritual significance.

üìñ Understanding the Prashnavali System

The Hanuman Prashnavali system operates on principles of divine numerology and spiritual vibrations. Each of the 49 messages carries unique energy and wisdom, carefully crafted to address various aspects of human life and spiritual growth.

The Sacred Process

The consultation process involves several key elements: - Mental purification and focus - Sacred invocation of Lord Hanuman - Contemplation of one's question - Selection of a number between 1 and 49 - Interpretation and implementation of the guidance received

üõï Proper Method of Consultation

1. Preparation

Before consulting the Prashnavali, one should: - Take a bath and wear clean clothes - Choose an auspicious time (preferably morning) - Sit facing East - Light a diya with ghee or sesame oil - Offer red flowers or sindoor to Lord Hanuman - Clear the mind of negative thoughts

2. Sacred Invocation

Begin with this powerful mantra: "Om Hanumate Namah" Recite the Hanuman Chalisa or at least these key verses: "Jai Hanuman gyan gun sagar Jai Kapis tihun lok ujagar"

3. Question Formulation

Guidelines for asking questions: - Keep questions clear and specific - Maintain pure intentions - Focus on spiritual growth and dharmic path - Avoid repetitive questioning - Ask with devotion and faith
